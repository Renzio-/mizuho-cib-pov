01 The Morning — Intelligence Without Effort ~2 min

It's 8:15am in Mizuho's Singapore office. Kenji Nakamura has a meeting with Sumitomo Corporation's Group Treasurer at 10am. He hasn't had time to prep — yesterday was consumed by a credit committee and three internal calls. He opens Salesforce.

The moment he lands on Sumitomo's Client 360 page, the work has already been done. The Banker Assistant has analyzed Sumitomo's entire portfolio overnight and surfaced three things Kenji needs to know:

On Screen
  • Client Briefing Panel loads automatically — three cards visible
  • Portfolio Signal: USD 200M bilateral loan maturing in 30 days
  • Covenant Alert: Debt/EBITDA ratio at 3.8x — approaching 4.0x threshold
  • Cross-Sell Trigger: FX hedge opportunity linked to the loan maturity
Talk Track
"Notice Kenji didn't search for anything. He didn't open a report. He didn't ask an analyst to prepare a brief. The system anticipated what he needs for today's meeting and surfaced it — proactively, automatically, before his first coffee."
02 The Deep-Dive — Conversational Intelligence ~2 min

The covenant alert catches Kenji's eye. He needs to understand the detail before walking into the room. Instead of digging through credit reports or calling the analyst team, he asks the agent directly.

Kenji types: "Tell me more about the covenant alert on Sumitomo."

On Screen
  • Agentforce copilot panel opens on the right
  • Agent responds with structured detail:
    • Covenant: Debt/EBITDA, threshold 4.0x
    • Current: 3.8x (up from 3.5x last quarter)
    • Trend: Deteriorating — driven by increased capex in mining division
    • Recommended talking point: "Discuss refinancing options before Q3 close"
  • Agent cites specific FinancialAccount and signal records — not hallucinating

Kenji then asks: "What FX products would be relevant for the loan maturity?"

On Screen
  • Agent responds with two product recommendations:
  • Option 1: 12-month JPY/USD forward — matches maturity timeline, hedges Sumitomo's USD exposure
  • Option 2: Cross-currency swap — better economics if they refinance in JPY
  • Both tied to Sumitomo's existing risk profile and prior FX history
Talk Track
"This is the shift from CRM as a system of record to CRM as a system of intelligence. Kenji didn't spend an hour pulling data from three systems. The agent connected the dots — loan maturity, covenant pressure, and hedging opportunity — in one conversation. That's time back for your RMs to spend with clients, not with spreadsheets."
— Kenji closes his laptop and walks to the meeting room at 9:55am —
03 The Meeting — Informed and Proactive ~30 sec (narrative only)

Kenji walks into the meeting informed, prepared, and proactive. He opens the conversation by referencing the loan maturity — something the Treasurer hadn't raised yet. He asks about their hedging appetite and mentions the FX forward option. The Treasurer is impressed: "You're ahead of us on this."

This is the moment the audience should feel: the RM is leading the conversation, not reacting to it. That's the shift from reactive to proactive. That's what Agentforce enables.

Talk Track
"We're not showing you what happened in the meeting — you know what good client meetings look like. What matters is that Kenji walked in with the right context, the right talking points, and the right product recommendations. He didn't need an analyst. He didn't need a pre-meeting call with product specialists. The agent did the prep."
04 Post-Meeting — Zero-Admin Capture ~2.5 min

Kenji returns to his desk at 11am. Normally this is where he'd spend 20 minutes filling in call reports, creating follow-up tasks, and sending emails. Instead, he opens the copilot and says:

"Capture meeting notes — we discussed the bilateral loan maturity and covenant situation. They're interested in a 12-month JPY/USD forward. Need to loop in S&T desk by Thursday. Next meeting in two weeks to present formal proposal."

On Screen
  • Agent confirms: "Meeting captured. Here's what I've done:"
  • Meeting Note: Created with structured summary, linked to Sumitomo + Treasurer contact
  • Task 1: "Send FX forward proposal to S&T desk" — assigned to S&T liaison, due Thursday
  • Task 2: "Prepare formal hedging proposal" — assigned to Kenji, due 2 weeks
  • Follow-up: Next meeting scheduled for June 29
  • Activity: Logged on Account timeline immediately

Kenji refreshes the Account page. The timeline shows the activity. The tasks are in his queue. The S&T desk has already received a Slack notification in the deal room channel.

On Screen (optional — Slack)
  • Slack deal room channel: automated message with meeting context
  • "Kenji met with Sumitomo re: loan maturity. Client interested in 12-month JPY/USD forward. Action needed by Thursday."
Talk Track
"Zero forms. Zero data entry. Kenji spoke naturally about his meeting and the system did the rest — structured notes, tasks with due dates, activity logged, S&T notified. This is what makes bankers actually want to use CRM. Not because they have to capture notes, but because the system captures them and does something intelligent with the information."
